## Non-Linear Editors (NLEs)

### Adobe Premiere Pro
- **Price**: $22.99/month (Creative Cloud)
- **Platform**: Windows, Mac
- **Strengths**: Industry standard, deep Creative Cloud integration (After Effects, Photoshop, Audition), multi-cam, Essential Sound panel, auto-captions, AI features (Generative Extend, Media Intelligence)
- **Weaknesses**: Subscription-only, can be resource-heavy, occasional stability issues
- **Best for**: Teams already in Adobe ecosystem, professional YouTube/podcast production
- **Used by**: Casey Neistat, Linus Tech Tips, many MrBeast editors

### DaVinci Resolve
- **Price**: Free / $295 one-time (Studio)
- **Platform**: Windows, Mac, Linux
- **Strengths**: Hollywood-grade color grading, all-in-one (Cut, Edit, Color, Fusion VFX, Fairlight audio with 2000+ tracks), supports up to 32K resolution, AI Neural Engine, real-time multi-user collaboration via Blackmagic Cloud
- **Weaknesses**: Steeper learning curve, free version lacks some AI features and GPU acceleration, heavy on GPU
- **Best for**: Color-critical work, budget-conscious creators, all-in-one workflow
- **Used by**: Hollywood films (Avatar, Green Book, Game of Thrones), growing YouTube adoption
- **Note**: The free version is genuinely professional software — not a demo. Best value in editing.

### Final Cut Pro
- **Price**: $299 one-time (Mac), $4.99/month (iPad)
- **Platform**: Mac only
- **Strengths**: Magnetic Timeline, optimized for Apple Silicon (fast renders), Object Tracker, automatic captions, one-time purchase with free updates
- **Weaknesses**: Mac-only, less third-party plugin ecosystem than Premiere
- **Best for**: Mac users, solo creators, fast turnaround workflows
- **Used by**: MKBHD, Peter McKinnon

### CapCut
- **Price**: Free / Pro subscription
- **Platform**: Windows, Mac, iOS, Android, Web
- **Strengths**: AI captions, templates, background removal, trending effects, 323M monthly active users. Dominant for short-form.
- **Weaknesses**: Limited for long-form, some features require Pro, less precise control
- **Best for**: Short-form content (Reels, TikTok, Shorts), beginners, quick edits
- **Used by**: Most Reels/TikTok creators

## Audio Tools

### Adobe Audition
- **Price**: $22.99/month
- **Strengths**: 128 tracks, 50+ effects, specialized audio restoration, noise reduction, spectral editing
- **Best for**: Podcast audio editing, noise cleanup, detailed audio work

### Avid Pro Tools
- **Price**: $29.99+/month
- **Strengths**: 256 tracks, 120+ effects, 64-bit engine, industry standard for music/film audio
- **Best for**: Professional audio post-production, music studios

### Logic Pro
- **Price**: Free with Mac
- **Strengths**: Unlimited tracks, 58 effects, Dolby Atmos, Spatial Audio
- **Best for**: Mac users doing music production and podcast audio

### REAPER
- **Price**: $60 (personal) / $225 (commercial)
- **Strengths**: Unlimited tracks, 34 built-in effects, tiny footprint (~20 MB), highly extensible with scripts/plugins
- **Best for**: Budget-conscious audio editors, power users who want customization

### Audacity
- **Price**: Free / open source
- **Strengths**: Multi-track, noise reduction, VST3/Nyquist plugins, cross-platform
- **Best for**: Quick audio edits, beginners, basic podcast editing

### Hindenburg Pro
- **Price**: ~$375 one-time
- **Strengths**: Purpose-built for spoken word, automatic leveling between speakers
- **Best for**: Podcast-focused workflows

## AI-Powered Tools

### Descript
- **Price**: $12+/user/month
- **Strengths**: Edit video by editing the text transcript, Studio Sound (AI audio enhancement), Overdub (AI voice cloning), automatic filler word removal, real-time collaboration
- **Best for**: Podcast editing, rough cuts, teams who want fast turnaround

### OpusClip
- **Price**: Free tier / paid plans
- **Strengths**: AI extracts best clips from long-form, virality scoring, automatic reframing to 9:16, 97%+ caption accuracy, B-roll insertion
- **Best for**: Automated podcast/YouTube clip creation for social media

### Submagic
- **Price**: Paid plans
- **Strengths**: AI auto-editing, captions in 48 languages, silence removal, B-roll suggestions, 4M+ businesses
- **Best for**: Batch short-form content creation

### Captions AI
- **Price**: Free tier / paid
- **Strengths**: Auto scene cutting, AI avatars from selfies, auto-captioning
- **Best for**: Solo creators wanting fast captioned content

### Auphonic
- **Price**: Free tier / paid
- **Strengths**: AI mastering, loudness normalization to -16 LUFS, de-esser, filler word removal
- **Best for**: Automated podcast audio mastering — "set it and forget it"

### Riverside
- **Price**: Various plans
- **Strengths**: Local 4K recording (not dependent on internet), AI editing, auto-layouts for multi-cam, Magic Clips for auto-clip extraction
- **Best for**: Remote podcast/interview recording + editing in one platform

## Motion Graphics and VFX

### Adobe After Effects
- **Price**: $22.99/month (Creative Cloud)
- **Strengths**: Industry standard for motion graphics, compositing, VFX. Huge plugin ecosystem. Deep Premiere integration.
- **Best for**: Lower thirds, title sequences, animated infographics, visual effects

### Fusion (Built into DaVinci Resolve)
- **Price**: Free (included)
- **Strengths**: Node-based compositing, 3D workspace, particle systems. Professional VFX without extra cost.
- **Best for**: DaVinci Resolve users who need motion graphics and VFX

### Apple Motion
- **Price**: $49.99 one-time
- **Strengths**: Real-time motion graphics, templates for Final Cut Pro
- **Best for**: Final Cut Pro users needing custom motion graphics

## Recording Tools

### OBS Studio
- **Price**: Free / open source
- **Best for**: Screen recording, live streaming, local video recording

### Riverside
- **Best for**: Remote podcast interviews with local recording quality

### Zencastr
- **Price**: Free tier / paid
- **Best for**: Remote podcast recording with separate audio tracks
