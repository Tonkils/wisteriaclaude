## The Hook (First 1-3 Seconds)

The hook decides everything. Techniques:
- **Direct statement**: Bold claim or provocative opinion. No buildup.
- **Question**: Create a curiosity gap the viewer needs answered.
- **Action start**: Jump straight into the most visually interesting moment.
- **Pattern interrupt**: Something unexpected — a weird visual, a sound, a rapid change.
- **Text hook**: Bold on-screen text with a question or teaser before speaking.

Never open with: title cards, slow intros, "hey guys," logos, or anything that doesn't immediately earn attention.

## Editing Rhythm and Pacing

- Cut every **2-3 seconds** minimum
- Remove ALL dead air — every millisecond of silence is a potential scroll
- Zoom punches on emphasis words (10-20% scale increase)
- Speed ramps: normal → fast → normal to skip boring parts
- Beat-sync cuts to music drops
- Front-load value in the first 3-5 seconds
- Target duration sweet spots: **15s, 30s, 60s, 90s**

## Safe Zones

```
┌─────────────────────┐
│   TOP 14% (250px)   │  ← Username, following button overlay
│                     │
│                     │
│   SAFE ZONE (66%)   │  ← Design your content here
│                     │
│                     │
│  BOTTOM 20% (340px) │  ← Caption/description overlay
└─────────────────────┘
```

Keep all critical text and visuals in the middle 66% of the frame.

## Transitions

| Transition | When to Use | How |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Zoom punch | Emphasis on a word or moment | 10-20% scale up, 0.1-0.2s |
| Whip pan | Scene change, energy boost | Fast pan blur between clips |
| Match cut | Sophistication, visual storytelling | Match shape/motion between clips |
| Jump cut | Remove dead air in talking head | Cut the gap, keep the energy |
| Speed ramp | Skip boring parts, add drama | 200-400% speed → snap to normal |
| Morph cut | Invisible cut in talking head | Premiere Pro / Resolve morph dissolve |
| Object reveal | Product showcase, surprise | Object fills frame → reveals next scene |
| Snap/clap sync | Music sync, rhythm | Cut on the beat, hand clap, or finger snap |

## Text and Captions

- **Kinetic typography**: Animate text synced to speech beats. Word-by-word reveals.
- **Highlight/underline**: Emphasize key words with color or underline animation.
- **Font**: Bold, sans-serif, high contrast against background. Minimum 40pt equivalent.
- **Shadow/outline**: Always add text shadow or outline for readability on varied backgrounds.
- **Placement**: Center-bottom of safe zone or center of frame. Never in the top 14% or bottom 20%.
- **Duration**: Each text element visible for 1-3 seconds maximum.

## Sound Design

- **Music**: 10-20% volume behind dialogue. Select before editing — cut to the beat.
- **Whoosh**: On transitions and fast camera moves
- **Impact/bass hit**: On zoom punches and emphasis moments
- **Riser**: Build tension before a reveal
- **Pop/ding**: On text appearing or key points
- **Trending sounds**: Use before they peak, not after. Check Reels audio library.
- **Test on phone speakers**: Most viewers watch on phone with no headphones.

## Color Grading

- High contrast, punchy saturation for small mobile screens
- Slightly warm skin tones
- Teal-and-orange cinematic grade remains widely used
- Check final output on a phone — desktop monitors lie about how it'll look mobile
- Consistency across a series matters more than any single grade

## Format Specs

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 (vertical) |
| Resolution | 1080x1920 minimum (1440x2560 recommended) |
| Frame rate | 30 fps standard, 60 fps for action |
| File size | Under 4 GB |
| Format | MP4 or MOV |
| Duration | Instagram Reels: up to 90s. TikTok: up to 10 min. Shorts: up to 60s. |
| Audio | AAC, 44.1 kHz |

## Content Archetypes

### Talking-Head Commentary (Hormozi / Chris Williamson Style)
- Kinetic text synced to speech
- Rapid jump cuts, no pauses
- Zoom punches on key words
- Emoji overlays for emphasis
- Bold caption animations
- Bass-boosted sound effects on transitions

### Product Showcase (Daniel Schiffer Style)
- Cinematic B-roll with sound design
- Macro shots, speed ramps
- Product interaction (hands, unboxing, pouring)
- Ambient sound + music sync
- Minimal text, maximum visual

### Cinematic Travel (Sam Kolder Style)
- Speed ramps, match cuts, hyperlapse
- Teal-orange color grade
- Drone footage mixed with ground-level
- Music-driven editing (cut to beat)
- Slow-motion for emotional moments

### Podcast Clips
- Animated captions always on
- Speaker reframed to center of 9:16
- Key quote highlighted as text overlay
- Hook = most provocative moment first
- CTA at end (full episode link)

### Comment Response (2025-2026 Trend)
- Screen-record or screenshot the comment
- Direct reply format drives engagement
- Quick, authentic, conversational

### Warmth + Wisdom (Jay Shetty / On Purpose Style)
Use for: TRA podcast clips, interview content, healing/personal development content, any brand where trust matters more than hype.
- **Hook**: Universal pain or a surprising insight — never a loud visual. Open mid-sentence if the first line is strong enough.
- **No jump cuts with sound design**: Clean silent cuts only — no whooshes, no bass hits. Silence is intentional.
- **Earthy caption font**: Warm tones, not neon. Bold but not aggressive. Key words can be larger — not flashing.
- **Protect emotional space**: If a guest pauses before saying something important, keep the pause. It's part of the clip.
- **Self-contained arc**: Hook → journey or context → payoff. Every clip delivers a complete thought.
- **Naming**: Title = the exact insight. "Why you can't stop attracting the wrong people" not "Episode 7 clip."
- **No branded intro on clips**: Start instantly. The first second IS the brand.

## 2025-2026 Trends

- Quality over quantity shift (66% of users more selective)
- Episodic series with multi-part hooks
- Shoppable Reels (up to 30 product tags)
- Talking-head dominance continues
- AI-assisted captioning as baseline expectation
- Behind-the-scenes content for authenticity
- Educational expert content over entertainment-only
- Nostalgia / Y2K aesthetics in visual style

## Workflow

1. **Select the moment** — hook-worthy, self-contained, emotional peak
2. **Trim** to platform duration sweet spot (15s, 30s, 60s, 90s)
3. **Reframe** to 9:16 with safe zones respected
4. **Cut** the pacing — every 2-3 seconds, remove all dead air
5. **Add kinetic text / captions** — synced to speech rhythm
6. **Sound design** — whooshes, risers, impacts on cuts, background music
7. **Color grade** — punchy, high contrast, check on phone
8. **Add CTA overlay** — follow, comment, share, link
9. **Export** and test on phone before posting
10. **Write caption, hashtags, select cover photo**
