## Editing Styles

### Retention Editing (The MrBeast School)

The dominant YouTube editing paradigm. Designed to maximize Average View Duration (AVD).

**Visual stimulation:**
- Punch-in zooms every 3-8 seconds (10-20% scale increase)
- Quick cuts eliminating all dead space
- Pop-up text reinforcing key words/numbers
- Meme inserts and reaction images (sparingly)
- Picture-in-picture overlays
- Camera angle switching via cropping
- Screen shake for emphasis
- Emoji overlays and animated stickers
- Bright, saturated color grading

**Audio stimulation:**
- Sound effects on nearly every visual change (whoosh, pop, ding, boom)
- Background music shifting with emotional beats
- Bass drops and risers for transitions
- Audio ducking: music drops under speech, rises during transitions

**Pacing rules:**
- Hook within first 5 seconds — teaser of the best moment or a bold promise
- No single shot holds longer than 5-8 seconds
- Every boring moment cut or covered with B-roll
- Pattern interrupts every 30-60 seconds (new angle, graphic, B-roll, meme)
- Stakes escalation throughout the video
- "Payoff loops": tease → build → deliver → tease next

**Text and graphics:**
- Bold animated text for key numbers and stats
- Progress bars, countdown timers for challenges
- Lower thirds for speaker identification
- Arrow graphics pointing to important details
- Split screens for comparisons

**Note:** MrBeast himself has acknowledged wanting to move past the "ultra fast-paced/overstim era" — the style is maturing. Best editors know when to use retention techniques and when to let content breathe.

### Cinematic Style (MKBHD / Casey Neistat)

**Color grading:**
- LUT-based correction as a starting point, then manual grade
- Cinematic color science: teal/orange, desaturated, or film emulation
- Shoot in LOG for maximum flexibility
- Tools: Lumetri Color (Premiere), Color page (DaVinci Resolve)

**Camera movement:**
- Slider shots, gimbal stabilization
- Shallow depth of field, rack focus pulls
- Motivated camera movement — every move has a purpose

**Slow motion:**
- 120 fps / 240 fps source for dramatic reveals
- Speed ramping: normal → slow-mo → normal
- Pair with music beat drops for maximum impact

**Transitions:**
- J-cuts and L-cuts for smooth scene changes (audio leads or trails the visual cut)
- Whip pans, match cuts
- Light leaks, lens flares as transition elements
- Masking transitions (hide cuts behind passing objects)

**Pacing:**
- Slower than retention editing — shots hold 5-15 seconds
- Let visuals breathe. Not every second needs stimulation.
- Wide → medium → close shot progression for establishing scenes

### Explainer Style (Johnny Harris / Kurzgesagt / Wendover)

- Motion graphics as primary visual (After Effects, Fusion)
- Map animations for geopolitical/logistics topics
- Custom diagrams, infographics, data visualization
- Screen recordings with zoom and highlight for tech topics
- Voiceover-first editing: cut visuals to narration, not the other way around
- Clean, branded visual identity
- Archival footage and photography for historical context

## YouTube Video Structure

```
0:00  — Hook (5-15 seconds: teaser, question, or bold promise)
0:15  — Context/Setup (explain why this matters)
1:00  — Main Content Section 1
       [Pattern interrupt / B-roll / graphic]
3:00  — Main Content Section 2
       [Pattern interrupt]
5:00  — Main Content Section 3
       [Midroll ad placement if monetized]
7:00  — Climax / Payoff
8:00  — Conclusion + CTA (subscribe, comment question, next video)
       End screen elements (last 20 seconds)
```

Use chapter markers: most 10-15 minute videos use 4-8 chapters. Chapters improve navigation, SEO, and viewer retention at section starts.

## Workflow (15-40 Editor Hours per Video)

### Phase 1: Ingest and Organize (1-3 hours)
- Transfer raw footage to SSD
- Organize into bins: A-cam, B-cam, B-roll, audio, graphics, music
- Generate proxy files if editing 4K+ on slower hardware
- Sync external audio to video
- Review footage and mark selects (best takes, key moments)

### Phase 2: Rough Cut (3-8 hours)
- Lay down script/narration as the foundation track
- Select best takes for each section
- Assemble main content in order
- Make major structural decisions (what stays, what goes)
- Establish rough timing — aim for target video length

### Phase 3: Fine Cut (4-10 hours)
- Refine every cut — tighten jump cuts, remove all dead space
- Place B-roll at appropriate moments
- Select and lay in music (match energy to content)
- Tighten pacing per the editing style chosen
- Add sound effects on transitions, zooms, emphasis moments
- Insert screen recordings, demonstrations, archival clips

### Phase 4: Graphics and Polish (2-6 hours)
- Lower thirds, text overlays, callouts
- Intro/outro sequences
- Subscribe CTAs and end screen elements
- Chapter markers in timeline
- Custom motion graphics from After Effects if needed
- Branded elements (logos, colors, fonts)

### Phase 5: Color and Audio (2-4 hours)
- Color correction: exposure, white balance, shot matching
- Color grading: creative look applied consistently
- Audio mixing: dialogue at -16 LUFS
- Music and SFX level balancing
- Noise reduction on dialogue tracks
- Final audio master

### Phase 6: Review and Revision (2-6 hours per round)
- Send cut to creator via Frame.io or Google Drive
- Receive notes and feedback
- Execute revisions (typically 1-3 rounds)
- Final approval from creator

### Phase 7: Export and Delivery (1-2 hours)
- Final export: H.264 or H.265, 4K preferred (1080p minimum), high bitrate
- Thumbnail: pull best frame or create custom design
- Description, tags, chapters, end screens, cards
- Upload and schedule

## Editor Responsibilities (Beyond Cutting)

A modern YouTube editor is effectively a one-person post-production house:

1. **Storytelling and pacing** — editorial judgment about what to cut is the highest-value skill
2. **Color correction and grading** — shot matching, creative LUTs, brand consistency
3. **Sound design and audio mixing** — SFX selection/timing, music editing, dialogue cleanup
4. **Motion graphics and text** — lower thirds, titles, basic After Effects work
5. **Thumbnail awareness** — edit should deliver on the thumbnail's promise
6. **Platform knowledge** — understanding CTR, AVD, algorithm signals, optimal video length
7. **Communication** — interpreting creative direction, managing revision cycles

## Key Metrics the Edit Affects

| Metric | What the Editor Controls |
|--------|--------------------------|
| Click-Through Rate (CTR) | Thumbnail-worthy moments, title-worthy hooks |
| Average View Duration (AVD) | Pacing, pattern interrupts, payoff loops |
| Retention curve | Hook strength, mid-video engagement, drop-off prevention |
| Comments | Engagement prompts, controversial moments, question CTAs |
| Shares | Emotional peaks, surprising reveals, relatable moments |
