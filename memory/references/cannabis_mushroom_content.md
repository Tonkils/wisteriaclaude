## Cannabis Content — What Works in 2026

### Best Performing Content Types
- **Education** — strain deep-dives, science of cannabis, debunking myths. Strongest for long-term trust
- **Lifestyle + recipes** — cannabis-infused cooking, daily routines, fashion integration
- **Product reviews & unboxings** — highly engaging, drives affiliate income
- **Authenticity/raw storytelling** — real stories dominate; audiences detect inauthenticity immediately
- **Behind-the-scenes** — grow tours, dispensary tours, team intros

### Platform Rules
- **Instagram/TikTok** — cannot directly sell or show product use. Use lifestyle framing, education, emojis (🍃💨) instead of flagged keywords. Direct sales = account ban
- **TikTok** — strictest. Even hemp/CBD gets flagged. Use #w33d, "oueed", avoid #cannabis
- **YouTube** — most lenient. Best for long-form reviews, grow journals, strain education, dispensary tours. Post 1-2x/week
- **Post 3-7x per week** across platforms to maintain algorithm visibility

### Strategy
- Build on YouTube (most lenient) and use Instagram/TikTok as discovery funnels
- No direct product links — use educational landing pages instead
- UGC (user-generated content) performs strongly on Instagram
- Focus on organic reach since mainstream ad platforms won't run cannabis ads

## Psilocybin/Mushroom Content — What Works in 2026

### Context
- 11 million U.S. adults used psilocybin in the past year — going mainstream
- Market moving faster than regulation; huge education gap = content opportunity
- Legal in Oregon (regulated therapeutic access), decriminalized in several cities

### Best Performing Content Angles
- **Harm reduction** — safest legally, most trusted by audiences
- **Microdosing + wellness** — huge with health-conscious audiences, mental health framing
- **Mycology education** — cultivation, species identification, science of fungi
- **Personal experience storytelling** — deeply viral when authentic
- **Integration and mental health** — therapeutic context, science-backed

### Platform Survival Tips
- TikTok bans "psychedelic" as a search term — use "mycology", "wellness", "fungi", "functional mushrooms"
- Frame everything as education or harm reduction, never promotion
- Avoid showing consumption directly
- Instagram more lenient than TikTok if framed as wellness/mental health
- Influencer marketing has fewer regulations than traditional advertising — most successful channel

### Top Creator Strategies
- Gabe Hardie (The Launchpad Podcast) — destigmatization through storytelling
- Harm reduction creators with 200k+ TikTok followers using "Bubble Rave" livestream format
- Red Headed Yeti — trip tips, drug chemistry, harm reduction

## Universal Principles for Both Topics

- Lead with **education + authenticity** — builds trust, protects accounts
- Avoid direct sales language on all platforms
- YouTube = main content hub (most lenient)
- Instagram/TikTok = discovery funnels with safe framing
- Community trust first, monetization second
- Corrections and behavior changes are highest priority memories
