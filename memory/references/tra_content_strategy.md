## What TRA Is

Trauma Relief Alliance (TRA) is a podcast hosted by Nina Boyd about psychedelic mushrooms (psilocybin), consciousness, therapy, transformation, and healing. Content must not get flagged on social platforms.

---

## TRA's Target Audience

- People dealing with depression, PTSD, anxiety, grief, addiction, childhood trauma
- People who have tried conventional therapy and medication and want alternatives
- Age: broad — 25-55+. NOT just young people. 88-year-old PTSD story was one of most shared pieces of content in this space
- Health-conscious, spiritually curious, personal development-oriented
- Psilocybin use up 44% among 18-29yo, 188% among 30+ (2019-2023)
- Growing mainstream interest — FDA may approve psilocybin for depression

---

## Content Pillars That Work

### 1. Personal Transformation Stories (Highest Viral Potential)
- Before/after narrative arcs: "30 years of depression. Gone after one session."
- Lead with the most dramatic outcome, not the backstory
- Audiences share these because they represent hope
- The hook IS the transformation

### 2. Science & Credibility (Flag Protection + Trust Building)
- Johns Hopkins studies, FDA acknowledgment, clinical trials
- Open every clip with research-backed statement
- Science-first framing keeps content alive on platforms
- Reference: Huberman Lab psilocybin episode = one of most viewed psilocybin videos ever

### 3. Specific Trauma Types (Niche = Reach)
Top performing angles:
- PTSD + veterans (Heroic Hearts Project has massive reach)
- Childhood trauma + abuse recovery
- Grief and loss
- Treatment-resistant depression
- Addiction recovery
- TBI/concussions (ex-NHL player Daniel Carcillo's story went viral)

### 4. Integration & "What Now?" Content
- "What happens AFTER a psilocybin session" performs very well
- Nervous system regulation, set and setting, microdosing protocols
- Practical guidance — audiences want hope AND actionable information

### 5. Controversy That Drives Comments
- "Why is this still illegal when it saved my life?"
- "Your doctor won't tell you this exists"
- "The FDA may approve psilocybin this year — here's what that means"

## Platform Strategy

### Instagram Reels (Primary Growth Channel)
- 30-60 second clips with cold open hook
- Styled captions for key terms: psilocybin, neuroplasticity, ego dissolution, set and setting
- Split screen reactions at emotional peaks
- Floating nature/spore/forest B-roll for visual variety
- Frame as: healing, therapy, wellness — NEVER recreation

### YouTube (Depth and Longevity)
- Full 30-60 min episodes
- Chapter-based structure: (1) Who is this person + why mushrooms, (2) Their story, (3) What they learned/integration
- Guest face + provocative question in thumbnail — NO mushroom imagery
- Run a clips channel alongside full episodes (name clips after the exact insight, not a generic title)
- Timestamps drive watch time and algorithm

### TikTok (Discovery Only — High Risk)
- Use: "nervous system healing," "trauma therapy," "mental health" — NOT "psilocybin" or "psychedelic"
- Text + voiceover safer than showing footage
- If flagged, immediately repurpose to Reels

## Editing Techniques (from Drive Podcast Content Plan)

Short form (Instagram):
1. Split-screen dual speaker — zoom into one face at emotional peaks
2. Floating B-roll overlays — mushroom timelapses, forest canopy, macro spore footage
3. Slow zoom in → instant snap cut on revelation moment
4. Styled caption typography — key terms bigger/bolder in earth tones
5. Cold open cinematic hook — most shocking sentence first + cinematic audio swell
6. Location/institution B-roll when speaker references Johns Hopkins, Oregon clinics, etc.
7. AI facial tracking auto-reframe for vertical conversion

Long form (YouTube):
- Clean talking-head, no visual distractions — credibility is in the guest
- Guest credential title cards at introduction
- Structured topic timestamps in description
- Invisible edits — no jump cuts with music stingers; clean silent cuts
- Ambient music under emotional transitions
- Science-first framing to open every episode
